Learn About Phishing

Phishing is a form of online scam where attackers trick users into revealing personal information such as passwords, OTPs, or credit card numbers by pretending to be trustworthy entities. Learn how to identify and avoid them.

How Phishing Works

Cybercriminals send fake messages that appear to come from legitimate sources. They often create a sense of urgency to make victims act quickly without verifying authenticity.

Example 1: “Your account will be suspended! Verify now.”

Clue: Creates unnecessary urgency to trick users.

Example 2: “Click this link to confirm your password.”

Clue: No legitimate company will ask for passwords or OTPs through links.

Checklist for Safety

  • ✅ Verify sender’s full address or phone number.
  • ✅ Hover over links before clicking.
  • ✅ Never share confidential details.
  • ✅ Contact the organization directly through official channels.
  • ✅ Report suspicious messages to your security team.
